## **1\. Introduction**

This document defines the **APTITUDE Presentation Profile** for interoperable credential verification across APTITUDE pilots.

It specifies a baseline, cross-pilot profile for the validation of trust artifacts aligned with the [APTITUDE Trust Specifications](https://aptitude-consortium.github.io/wp2-trust-specifications/latest/), the [references](https://aptitude-consortium.github.io/wp2-trust-specifications/latest/deliverable-2.1-trust/#references_3) therein, and the ARF.

The purpose of this RFC is to establish a stable starting point for interoperability across APTITUDE pilots by defining:

- impacted roles and components;
- normative requirements for entities involved in the validation of WRPAC and WRPRC certificates;
- interface definitions for the relevant protocols (OCSP, CRL, Status List Token);
- a conformance check catalogue that maps the normative requirements into verifiable checks;
- a test case matrix that maps the conformance checks into executable test cases for the **ITB+ conformance test cases**.

This RFC is intended to support interoperability first. Pilot-specific or sector-specific extensions may be added later, but they should not weaken the baseline requirements defined here.

- **Trust Anchor Validation** -- profiled for the validation of LoTE and EUMS LoTL as defined in ETSI TS 119 615 and [Trust Anchor Validation](https://aptitude-consortium.github.io/wp2-trust-specifications/latest/deliverable-2.1-trust/#trust-anchor-validation-process);

## **4\. Roles and Components**

### **4.1 Wallet Relying Party**

The **<roles:Wallet Relying Party>**, as defined in the APTITUDE glossary and aligned with the ARF notions of Relying Party, Attestation Provider and PID Provider. In this RFC, it is the entity to whom the WRPAC and WRPRC are issued.  

### **4.2 Wallet Unit**

The **Wallet Unit** is the <components:Wallet Unit> as defined in the ARF and APTITUDE glossary, including one or more <components:Wallet Instance|Wallet Instances> on the Holder’s device or environment. In this RFC, the term **Wallet** refers to the Wallet Unit (and its Wallet Instances) when the distinction is not material, acting on behalf of the <roles:Holder> to verify the trustworthiness of a WRP by evaluating trust with the WRPAC and WRPRC received when transacting with a WRP.

### **4.3 Provider of WRPAC**

The **<roles:Provider of WRPAC>**, as defined in the APTITUDE glossary and aligned with the ARF notion of Provider of WRPAC, is the entity issuing WRPAC and the related status Services (CRL endpoint and/or OCSP responder).

### **4.4 Provider of WRPRC**

The **<roles:Provider of WRPRC>**, as defined in the APTITUDE glossary and aligned with the ARF notion of Provider of WRPRC, is the entity issuing WRPRC and the related status Services (Status List Token endpoint).

### **4.5 LoTE Scheme Operator**

The **<roles:LoTE Scheme Operator>, as defined in the APTITUDE glossary and aligned with the ETSI notion of LoTE Scheme Operator, is the entity responsible for the management of the LoTE Trusted List and the inclusion of the Trust Anchors for the applicable entities.

### **4.6 EUMS TL Scheme Operator**

The **<roles:EUMS TL Scheme Operator>, as defined in the APTITUDE glossary and aligned with the ETSI notion of EUMS TL Scheme Operator, is the entity responsible for the management of the EUMS Trusted List and the inclusion of the Trust Anchors for the applicable entities.

### **4.7 Registrar**

The **<roles:Registrar>**, as defined in the APTITUDE glossary and aligned with the ARF notion of Registrar, is the entity responsible for the publication and management of the **<role:Registrar>**.

## **6\. Normative Requirements**

This section defines the baseline normative requirements for APTITUDE implementations of the issuance profile.

Unless otherwise stated, the requirements in this section apply to issuance interactions only.

### **6.1 Wallet Requirements**

A Wallet conforming to this RFC SHALL:

- Support ETSI TS 119 615 for Trust Anchor validation;
- Support RFC 5280 for certificate path validation;
- Support [Authorization Validation Procedures](https://aptitude-consortium.github.io/wp2-trust-specifications/latest/deliverable-2.1-trust/#evaluation-procedures) for the WRPRC and Register response validation.


### **6.2 WRP Requirements**

A WRP conforming to this RFC SHALL:

- Possess a valid WRPAC certificate issued by a trusted Provider of WRPAC, and [OPTIONALLY] a valid WRPRC certificate issued by a trusted Provider of WRPRC;

### **6.3 Provider of WRPAC/WRPRC Requirements**

A Provider of WRPAC/WRPRC conforming to this RFC SHALL:

- be listed in the appropriate LoTE together with its Trust Anchor certificate in the `ServiceDigitalIDentity` field;

### **6.4 LoTE/EUMS TL Scheme Operator Requirements**

A LoTE Scheme Operator conforming to this RFC SHALL:

- expose a LoTE endpoint as described in section [LoTE Endpoint](#71-lote-endpoint);

A EUMS TL Scheme Operator conforming to this RFC SHALL:

- expose a LoTL/EUMS TL endpoint as described in section [LoTL/EUMS TL Endpoint](#72-lotleums-tl-endpoint);

### **6.4 Registrar Requirements**

A Registrar conforming to this RFC SHALL:

- be listed in the appropriate LoTE together with its Trust Anchor certificate in the `ServiceDigitalIDentity` field;
- expose a Register endpoint as described in section [Register Endpoint](#73-register-endpoint);

## **7\. Interface Definitions**

This section defines the logical interfaces for conformance testing. The following interfaces are added on top of these added in RFC001, RFC002.

### **7.1 LoTE Endpoint**

- **Direction**: Wallet to LoTE Scheme Operator.
- **Method**: `GET`

#### **Request**

The Wallet SHALL construct an HTTP `GET` request to retrieve the LoTE associated with the certificate's Trust Anchor.

- **URI Construction**:

  - The request URI is extracted directly from the 
  - No additional query parameters are required.

- **Headers**:

  - `Host`: SHALL contain the hostname from the LoTE URI.
  - `Accept`: SHALL contain the value `application/json`.

#### **Response**

The LoTE Scheme Operator endpoint SHALL respond with the appropriate HTTP status codes and the correctly formatted LoTE data if the request is successful.

- **Status Codes**:

  - `200 OK`: The request was successful, and the LoTE data is included in the response body.
  - `4xx` / `5xx`: Standard HTTP error codes (e.g., `404 Not Found`, `500 Internal Server Error`) SHALL be used only if the server cannot process the HTTP request at the transport level.

- **Headers**:

  - `Content-Type`: SHALL be set to `application/json` for successful responses containing the LoTE data.
  - `Content-Length`: SHOULD be included to indicate the size of the LoTE data in bytes.

- **Body**:

  - The response body SHALL contain the LoTE data in JSON format as specified in ETSI TS 119 612.

### **7.2 LoTL/EUMS TL Endpoint**

- **Direction**: Wallet to LoTL/EUMS TL Scheme Operator.
- **Method**: `GET`

#### **Request**

The Wallet SHALL construct an HTTP `GET` request to retrieve the LoTL/EUMS TL associated with the certificate's Trust Anchor.

- **URI Construction**:

  - The request URI is extracted directly from the 
  - No additional query parameters are required.

- **Headers**:

  - `Host`: SHALL contain the hostname from the LoTL/EUMS TL URI.
  - `Accept`: SHALL contain the value `application/xml`.

#### **Response**

The LoTL/EUMS TL Scheme Operator endpoint SHALL respond with the appropriate HTTP status codes and the correctly formatted LoTL/EUMS TL data if the request is successful.

- **Status Codes**:

  - `200 OK`: The request was successful, and the LoTL/EUMS TL data is included in the response body.
  - `4xx` / `5xx`: Standard HTTP error codes (e.g., `404 Not Found`, `500 Internal Server Error`) SHALL be used only if the server cannot process the HTTP request at the transport level.

- **Headers**:

  - `Content-Type`: SHALL be set to `application/xml` for successful responses containing the LoTL/EUMS TL data.
  - `Content-Length`: SHOULD be included to indicate the size of the LoTL/EUMS TL data in bytes.

- **Body**:

  - The response body SHALL contain the LoTL/EUMS TL data in XML format as specified in ETSI TS 119 602.

### **7.3 Register Endpoint**

- **Direction**: Wallet Unit to National Register API.
- **Method**: `GET`
- **Authentication**: None (Open for public access without prior authentication).

The Wallet Unit utilizes the public endpoints of the national common REST API to query and verify the registration status, declared entitlements, and registered intended uses of a WRP.

#### **Request**

The Registry API exposes two public `GET` endpoints that the Wallet Unit may invoke depending on the verification context:

- `GET /wrp` **(Search / List)**: Used to fetch full registry data matching specific query criteria. If no query parameters are provided, it returns the full list of registered WRPs subject to pagination profiles.
- `GET /wrp/check-intended-use` **(Intended Use Check)**: A dedicated, narrowed-down endpoint used to explicitly verify whether a specific transaction profile is registered for a given WRP.

- **URI Construction**:

  - The base request URI is extracted from the registry configuration metadata (e.g., the `registryURI` field or relevant Trust List service locations).
  - For `GET /wrp`, the endpoint supports parameterized queries using combined filters. Supported query parameters include:
    
    - `identifier`: Filter by official business registration number.
    - `legalname`: Filter by official company name.
    - `tradename`: Filter by user-facing trade name.
    - `policy`: Filter by privacy policy URL.
    - `entitlement`: Filter by entitlement type normative URI.
    - `intendeduseidentifier`: Filter by the registrar-assigned intended-use identifier.
    - `credentialformat` / `credentialmeta` / `claimpath`: Filter by specific requested attestation criteria.
    - `providesattestation`: Filter by attestation types provided.
    - `cursor` / `limit`: For cursor-based pagination handling.

  - For `GET /wrp/check-intended-use`, the parameters include:

    - `rpidentifier`: REQUIRED. The identifier of the WRP being checked.
    - `intendeduseidentifier`: OPTIONAL. The registered identifier for the specific intended use.
    - `credentialformat` / `credentialmeta` / `claimpath`: OPTIONAL parameters to check specific data request permissions.
    - `policyurl`: OPTIONAL parameter to check if the explicit privacy policy URL is registered for the WRP.

- **Headers**:

  - `Host`: SHALL contain the hostname from the `ServiceSupplyPoints` URI.
  - `Accept`: SHALL contain the value `application/jwt`.

#### **Response**

- **Status Codes**:

  - `200 OK`: The query was successfully received, evaluated, and resolved. A valid cryptographically signed statement is returned in the response body (even if the search filters result in an empty array or a boolean `false` check).
  - `400 Bad Request`: The request payload or query parameters were structurally invalid or incomplete.
  - `404 Not Found`: Used specifically by `/wrp/{identifier}` or `/wrp/check-intended-use` if the targeted WRP identifier does not exist in the register dataset.
  - `5xx`: Standard HTTP error codes SHALL be used if the registry service cannot process the request at the infrastructure or application layer.

- **Headers**:

  - `Content-Type`: SHALL be set to `application/jwt`.
  - `Content-Length`: SHOULD accurately reflect the size of the DER-encoded OCSP response in bytes.
  - `Cache-Control` / `Expires`: MAY be included to govern HTTP caching behaviors, in case it SHALL align with the `exp` field provided in the internal payload.

- **Body Format**:

  - The HTTP response body SHALL contain a JWS compact serialization string (Media Type: `application/jwt`) electronically signed or sealed by or on behalf of the Registrar.
  - When parsing the decoded JSON payload from a `GET /wrp` response, the statement containing the matching `WalletRelyingParty` data object array SHALL completely exclude the `postalAddress` field (which is restricted to the registrar administration view only). All other data schema parameters (including trade names, entitlements, and intended use structures) are visible as registered.
  - For a `GET /wrp/check-intended-use` response, the decoded JWS payload resolves to a compact boolean representation (`true` or `false`) or its profiled envelope equivalent indicating compliance.
